Animal Facts!

A

Aardvark

  1. Their closest living relative is the elephant.
  2. The average life span is 23 years.
  3. They weigh 117-181 lbs.
  4. Aardvarks can eat about 50,000 ants and termites.
  5. When they abandond there home, there predators live in it.

B

Bactrian Camel

  1. Camels chew in a figure 8 moition.
  2. When they feel threatened camels spit.
  3. They can go six months without eating.
  4. They can drink up to 5 gallons in one sitting.
  5. Bactrian Camels only have one hump.

Betta

  1. Every betta has a different tail shape.
  2. Bettas are extremely territorial.
  3. They live in tropical waters.
  4. There average lifespan is five years.
  5. If two bettas fight it usually leads to serious injury but it can sometimes lead to death.

E

elephant

  1. Elephants can live for up to 70 years.
  2. They eat about 16 hours each day.
  3. They have about 100,000 different muscles in there trunk.
  4. A bull elephants ear weighs over 100 pounds.
  5. Elephants have no sweat glands.

K

Kangaroos

  1. Male Kangaroos fight over the females by boxing eachother out.
  2. They watch there baby kangaroos for only about 7 months.
  3. Baby kangaroos are called joeys.
  4. They are mammals
  5. They are marsupials

S

Saltwater crocodile

  1. It is the largest living reptile.
  2. The average weight is 1,000 pounds.
  3. The average length is 17 ft.
  4. Most live over 65 years.
  5. They eat almost anything in there territory.
